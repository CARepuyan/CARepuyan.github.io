Flaves of The North

Patar Beach, Bolinao

Bolinao is known for a number of pristine beaches, the most popular of which is Patar Beach, situated on the western coast along the South China Sea southwest of the town proper. Patar Beach is a creamy white sand beach that slopes gradually from shore making it good for swimming. There are several more stretchers of white sandy beach with coral rock formatioins and small coves as you head further down south along the coast. This coastline includes stretches of nice beaches in the towns of Agno, Bani, Burgos and Dasol. Read More

Hundered Islands, Pangasinan

Hunderd Island epitomizes the best of Pangasinan, riddled by 123 islets spearing out of cerulean sea. Home of a staggering variety of wildlife and fishes, these coral islands make up the Hundered Islands National Park. Most of them deslate, save for the seven major islands that are usually included in the Island Hopping tour: Quezon Island, Governor’s Island, Marcos Island, Children’s Island, Virgin Island, Pilgrimage/Ramos Island (the one with large Jesus statue) and Scouts Island. Sabang Beach, Baler, Aurora

Surfing in the Philippines began in Baler. This small surfing town is located in the central part of the Aurora province, east of Luzon, and is a popular destination for travelers because of its sensational waves. Baler presents a quaint and laid-back vibe suitable for those looking for cozy beach resorts near Manila. Through the years, Baler has maintained its place, being one of the top attractions in the country. Aside from being a surfing destination, it is also home to historical attractions and breathtaking landscapes. Read More

How to get there?


Manila to Patar Beach, Bolinao

There are several buses that ply the Manila to Bolinao route namely Victory Liner, Five Star, Dagupan Bus, City Trans and Philippine Rabbit. Travel time is roughly around six hours. To head to Patar Beach simply rent a trike in Bolinao town proper.

If you’re going by car take the North Luzon Expressway (NLEX), then the Subic-Clark-Tarlac Expressway (SCTEX) towards Tarlac City. At Tarlac City take the road going to Camiling and drive all the way to Alaminos, Pangasinan. At Alaminos continue along the national highway until you reach the junction at the next town of Bani. Take the road going north to Bolinao. This road goes all the way to Bolinao town proper.

Manila to Hundered Islands, Pangasinan

Take a bus bound to Alaminos in Cubao either via Victory Liner, 5 Star Bus, Philippine Rabbit and Dagupan Bus. There are daily trips to Alaminos. Fare is about 400 pesos for AC bus and around 350 pesos for ordinary bus. Travel time is approx 5 hrs.


From Alaminos, Pangasinan bus terminal go to Lucap Terminal in front of Suki supermarket and take a tricycle to Lucap Wharf. Tricycle fare is about 20 pesos per person or you can charter the tricycke to Lucap Wharf for approx 100 pesos.

Sabang Beach, Baler

From bus terminals at EDSA Pasay in Manila, ride a bus bound for Baler. If you’re traveling via private car, traverse the North Luzon Expressway (NLEX), take the Subic–Clark–Tarlac Expressway (SCTEX) until you reach the Nueva Ecija-Aurora highway, the Aurora border, and finally, Baler.
