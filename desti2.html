Bantayan Island, Cebu

Bantayan Island is an off-the-beaten-path tourist destination in Cebu and is said to be the Boracay of the south! There are so many awesome things to do in Bantayan Island including white sand beaches, saltwater lagoons, mangrove boardwalks, cave pools, old ruins, skydiving, and island hopping, to name a few. How to get there?


Panglao Island from Manila

From Manila, you can book a direct flight going to Panglao International Airport.

Tagbilaran City to Panglao Island From Tagbilaran, you can ride a tricycle, a taxi or van, or a bus going to Panglao. The rates depend on the mode of transportation but the cheapest way to travel to Panglao is by bus.

By tricycle You can ride a tricycle in the city and ask the driver to drop you off directly to your hotel in Panglao. The fare could go around PHP 200-300 per ride.

By minivan/taxi From Tagbilaran ferry pier, you can ride a minivan or taxi for a more comfortable ride but it’s more expensive.

By bus From Tagbilaran ferry pier, ride a tricycle to Dao bus terminal. Upon arrival, take the Southern Star Bus which is bound for Panglao Airport and Alona Beach. Don’t worry, it only takes less than an hour, and the buses are air-conditioned so you will have a comfortable journey.

Bantayan Island from Manila

via AIR There are flights to Cebu from major cities in the Philippines, and international airports in South Korea, Taiwan, Singapore, Japan, Hong Kong, and Malaysia. It only takes 1 hour to reach Cebu from Manila by plane.

From the airport in Cebu, you still have to either hop on a bus and a Roll-On Roll-Off (RoRo), which is around 5-6 hours or take a 40-minute connecting flight to Bantayan Airport. Here are your travel options to Bantayan Island in detail when you arrive in Cebu City:


North Bus Terminal & Ferry Once you’re in Cebu’s Mactan International Airport, ride the MyBus shuttle to the North Bus Terminal. You can board a direct bus to Santa Fe in Bantayan, which will include onboard the RoRo. Or take the bus that will drop you off at Hagnaya Port where you will bring your luggage and get on board the RoRo. Fare is PHP160 to PHP200.

The buses at the terminal leave every hour, day and night, and travel time is around 4 hours. The RoRo ride is about 1.5 hours and costs PHP170. When you arrive in Santa Fe, you need to pay PHP 30 for the green fee charged to all visitors or non-residents.
