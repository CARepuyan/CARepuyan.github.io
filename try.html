Flaves of The North

Anguib Beach, Sta. Ana

Anguib Beach is deemed as the “Boracay of the North” as it is known for its white sand beach and picturesque sceneries — which makes it comparable to Boracay - One of the Worlds Best Beach Destination. The beach is surrounded by small mountains which make it even more majestic. This beautiful place is seated in Barangay San Vicente in the town of Sta. Ana. Read More

Nangaramoan, Sta. Ana

One of the many breathtaking places, one of it is Nangaramoan Beach. From its crystal blue water, rock formations and white coral beach, to the lush green forest, the place is just screaming beauty. Read More

Palaui Island, Sta. Ana

Fall in love in a place built by wind and waves, and nurtured by time; discover the distinct charm of its scenic ragged terrain; sing with the howling sound of its gigantic waves. Discover, explore and experience Palaui Island. Palaui Island is famous for scuba diving, snorkeling and fishing. Read More

How to get there?


via Bus
  1. Ride a bus heading to Tuguegarao from Caloocan, Sampaloc, or Kamias. Fare is around 600 pesos. Once in Tuguegarao,
  2. Then from Tuguegarao, ride a van going to Sta. Ana. Travel time is 3 hours and fare is 250 pesos. You can also ride the GMW bus directly to Sta. Ana, Cagayan from Baguio. Travel time is 14.5 hours and fare is 885 pesos. Both buses have aircon.
  3. Once in Santa Ana, ride a tricycle going to San Vicente Port for 10-20 minutes. The fare is 15-25 pesos per head.
  4. At the port, ride a boat going to Palaui Island. Travel time is 20-30 minutes depending on how big the waves are
via Plane

Aside from buses, there are also planes going to Tuguegarao. You can book online. From the airport, walk just a few meters to reach the highway leading to Sta. Ana, Cagayan. Just cross the street and wait for buses or vans to Sta Ana.
