Palaui Island, Sta Ana

White sandy beaches and coves, a century old lighthouse, hidden waterfalls and lovely people, all in a scenic ragged terrain surrounded by howling wind and huge waves, that is Palaui Island. With a land area of more than 3000 hectares, Palaui, an island made up of volcanic debris, is politically under the jurisdiction of the town of Sta. Ana. It is home to a fishing community, without electricity and commercial accommodation. Its main attraction aside from its beaches is the Farol de Cabo Engaño, an 18th century lighthouse built by the Spaniards that guides ships entering and leaving the coastal towns of Sta. Ana specially those vessels using Port Irene and San Vicente Port, and those traversing the Babuyan Channel and Philippine Sea. Fall in love in a place built by wind and waves, and nurtured by time; discover the distinct charm of its scenic ragged terrain; sing with the howling sound of its gigantic waves. Discover, explore and experience Palaui Island. Palaui Island is famous for scuba diving, snorkeling and fishing.

Anguib Beach, Sta Ana

Anguib Beach is deemed as the “Boracay of the North” as it is known for its white sand beach and picturesque sceneries — which makes it comparable to Boracay - One of the Worlds Best Beach Destination. The beach is surrounded by small mountains which make it even more majestic. This beautiful place is seated in Barangay San Vicente in the town of Sta. Ana.

Nangaramoan, Sta. Ana

Santa Ana Cagayan is a land full of opportunities. It is not only known for its online casino’s but also of the many breathtaking places, one of it is Nangaramoan Beach. From its crystal blue water, rock formations and white coral beach, to the lush green forest, the place is just screaming beauty. If you’ve been to Anguib beach before, Nangaramoan is just beside it. When Anguib was made private by the owners, Nangaramoan was opened to the public. No entrance fees are collected, you just need to pay for the cottages if you want to use one and also if you want to use their restrooms.

How to get here?

From Manila
Via Bus
  1. Ride a bus heading to Tuguegarao from Caloocan, Sampaloc, or Kamias. Fare is around 600 pesos. Once in Tuguegarao,
  2. Then from Tuguegarao, ride a van going to Sta. Ana. Travel time is 3 hours and fare is 250 pesos. You can also ride the GMW bus directly to Sta. Ana, Cagayan from Baguio. Travel time is 14.5 hours and fare is 885 pesos. Both buses have aircon.
  3. Once in Santa Ana, ride a tricycle going to San Vicente Port for 10-20 minutes. The fare is 15-25 pesos per head.
  4. At the port, ride a boat going to Palaui Island. Travel time is 20-30 minutes depending on how big the waves are
Via Plane
  • Aside from buses, there are also planes going to Tuguegarao. You can book online. From the airport, walk just a few meters to reach the highway leading to Sta. Ana, Cagayan. Just cross the street and wait for buses or vans to Sta Ana.
